## B) Archive research packet (for you or proxy researcher)

## Mission objective

Verify whether the 1891 act was:

- **surname/predicate addition only** (civil branding move), or
- **actual succession/recognition of noble title transfer**.

## Core research questions

1. Does the decree language grant only “aggiunta del cognome/predicato”?
2. Is there any explicit phrase asserting extinction of Prati male line?
3. Is there an attached genealogy and does it omit known U.S. heirs?
4. Did any subsequent registry classify “Marchese di Rovagnasco” as estinto vs dormiente?

## Priority source stack (in order)

1. **Gazzetta Ufficiale del Regno d’Italia** (Dec 1891–Mar 1892 notices).
2. **Consulta Araldica files** (petition dossier + legal rationale).
3. **Libro d’Oro / Elenco Ufficiale Nobiliario** entries across editions.
4. **State archives (Alessandria/Torino)** for linked lineage and property transitions.

## Search terms (Italian)

- “Regio Decreto 22 dicembre 1891 Pellati Rovagnasco”
- “aggiunta di cognome Rovagnasco”
- “Consulta Araldica Pellati”
- “Prati di Rovagnasco estinta / dormiente”
- “predicato di Rovagnasco cognomizzazione”

## Evidence capture standard

For each document captured, log:

- archive + fonds + series + box + file;
- exact quote (Italian) + English translation;
- scan/photo filename;
- why it supports/contradicts extinction claim;
- confidence rating (High/Med/Low).

## Decision matrix

- **If decree = name addition only:** title transfer not proven; lineage challenge pathway strengthens.
- **If decree = explicit succession:** evaluate legal basis and heir omission evidence.
- **If no extinction language found:** challenge relies on administrative overreach/assumption.

## C) Major financial and legal technical targets for possible real recovery

> This is the high-value lane scan: what can still be actionable versus symbolic.

## 1) Property chain-of-title reconstruction (highest practical value)

- Pull **catasto storico** entries for Palazzo/estate parcels.
- Pull **notarial deeds** for sales, partitions, mortgages, creditor actions.
- Build transfer timeline: owner → legal act → consideration → encumbrances.
- Outcome: determines whether loss was private sale, forced liquidation, or public transfer.

## 2) Succession law posture (title vs patrimony separated)

- Treat **noble dignity claims** and **real property rights** as separate tracks.
- Even if title is dormant/symbolic, property claims usually depend on deed law and prescription periods.
- Outcome: avoids sinking effort into non-recoverable feudal narrative when documentary equity route may remain.

## 3) Fraud/omission theory stress-test

- Compare petition assertions vs known heir existence in U.S. vital records.
- Identify whether omission was material under period standards.
- Outcome: likely strongest for **historical correction**; weaker for direct monetary recovery unless linked to a still-open asset path.

## 4) Unclaimed/abandoned assets sweep (Italy + U.S. + cross-border)

- Banking dormancy registries, escheat pipelines, municipal indemnity funds, dormant securities if any family trust remnants existed.
- Outcome: low probability, but non-zero and financially cleaner than title litigation.

## 5) Commercial exploitation/value today

- If recovery impossible: convert verified lineage archive into controlled IP product (licensed dossier, docu rights, publication rights, heritage consultancy offering).
- Outcome: transforms proof into modern revenue while preserving historical integrity.

## Triage recommendation

1. Decree + petition file (truth hinge)
2. Chain-of-title on key properties (economic hinge)
3. Heir-proof packet (identity hinge)
4. Legal memo on prescription/viability (go/no-go hinge)

## Minimal operating doctrine

- No claim without document ID.
- No narrative leap without two-source corroboration.
- Distinguish **mythic inheritance** from **recoverable rights** at every step.
- Preserve dignity of line, but litigate only where paper and time limits permit.
