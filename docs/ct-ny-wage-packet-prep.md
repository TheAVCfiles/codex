# CT/NY Wage Packet Preparation Kit

## Purpose

This document captures a courtroom-ready packet structure for two filing tracks:

1. **CT Department of Labor wage complaint packet** (primary enforcement path).
2. **NY Department of Labor complaint packet** (for documented NY nexus).

The packet design is intentionally neutral, evidence-first, and conservative in monetary estimates.

### CT packet scope

- Focus on CT-based work (e.g., Ridgefield studio / Wilton operations).
- Focus on misclassification, unpaid wages, overtime, and documented economic harm.
- Exclude speculative or non-wage issues (save for separate civil/agency workflows).

### CT ledger targets

- Hours worked estimate: ~1,600 hours.
- Conservative wage estimate: ~$200,000 owed.
- Include 2021 1099-NEC (`$17,841`) with one-page explanatory note.
- Include displacement and liquidation support exhibits.

## Displacement Ledger schema (`06_DISPLACEMENT_LEDGER.xlsx`)

For each line item, include:

- `Move #`
- `Date Range`
- `Location (City, State)`
- `Category`
- `Vendor`
- `Amount (USD)`
- `Proof Type`
- `Filename`
- `Verified?`

### Suggested categories

- Security deposit
- Emergency short-term housing
- Moving logistics
- Storage
- Application fees / credit-related costs

### Verification rule

- Mark unsupported rows as `UNVERIFIED` until documentary proof is attached.
- Include per-move subtotal and grand total target (`$46,800`).

## One-page `START_HERE` template (copy into PDF)

### Header

- Complainant name
- Mailing address or P.O. box
- Contact email/phone
- Date

### Employer section

- Employer names
- Employer addresses

### Complaint summary (neutral)

- Work period
- Duties performed
- Misclassification and control factors
- Pay received vs hours worked
- Resulting financial harms

### Requested relief

- Back wages
- Overtime
- Liquidated damages (where applicable)
- Agency investigation into classification/pay practices

### Attachments list

- List exhibits in the exact ZIP order.
- Confirm records are available for review.
- Add signature line.

## Filing strategy notes

1. File CT packet first when CT contacts/worksite/control evidence is strongest.
2. File NY packet when NY nexus evidence is sufficient and document-backed.
3. Reuse shared evidence exhibits but keep each packet tailored to venue standards.
4. Keep language factual and restrained to preserve credibility with agency intake staff.

## Add-on Module: DOL + COVID-period wage theft support

Use this module if the unpaid labor period overlapped with 2020â€“2022 operations or any period affected by pandemic scheduling disruptions.

### Add these exhibits to both CT and NY packets (when available)

- `11_COVID_SCHEDULING_AND_CONTROL_LOG.pdf`
- `12_PUBLIC_HEALTH_ERA_WORK_CONDITIONS_DECLARATION.pdf`
- `13_REVENUE_CONTINUITY_VS_PAY_GAP_SUMMARY.pdf`

### What to document in the COVID scheduling log

For each event, capture:

- Date and time
- Work request channel (text/email/call)
- Task type (teaching, production, transport, child-facing supervision, digital production)
- Whether task was same-day or emergency
- Whether compensation was offered/paid
- Evidence filename

### Revenue continuity vs pay gap summary

Build a one-page chart that compares:

1. Ongoing operations (classes, showcases, camps, performances, digital products).
2. Worker labor demand (hours, duties, role expansion).
3. Actual wages paid (or lack of pay).

This is useful for agency reviewers because it ties work performed to commercial continuity while showing wage non-payment.

## Add-on Module: DV/coercive-control context for wage enforcement

When coercion or safety threats affected ability to refuse labor, include a **fact-only context addendum** without changing the wage-first focus.

### Optional attachment

- `14_DV_COERCIVE_CONTROL_CONTEXT_ADDENDUM.pdf`

### Include only objective facts

- Dates of threatening or controlling communications.
- How those communications affected work compliance (e.g., inability to refuse shifts/tasks).
- Any documented restrictions on movement, housing, transportation, or communications.
- Link each statement to a message ID, screenshot filename, or third-party record.

### Keep venue-safe framing

- Avoid diagnosis language.
- Avoid speculative motive statements.
- Use: "This context is provided to explain why labor was performed under pressure and without normal negotiation power."
